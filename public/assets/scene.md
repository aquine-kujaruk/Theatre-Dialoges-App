# The Dining Room — Birthday Party Scene

---

**PEGGY**
All right, [excited] Children! [overwhelmed] We're ready!

**PEGGY**
Children, [firmer] children, [shouts] CHILDREN!

**PEGGY**
This is a dining room! [sharp] This is not the monkey house at the zoo!

**PEGGY**
All right then. [calm, threatening] I'll just have to tell Roberta in the kitchen to put away all the ice cream and cake.

**PEGGY**
[relieved] Good. That's much better. Now I want everyone to leave the table... [hushed] quietly, [firm] QUIETLY... And go into the hall, and then come back in here in the right way. That's it. Go out. Turn around. And come in. [warm] Come in as if you were your mummies and daddies coming into a lovely dinner party.

**PEGGY**
No, no. Let Winkie go first, since it's her birthday and she's the hostess... That's it. [encouraging] Good. Good. You sit at the head of the table, Winkie. Good. [redirecting] No, no, Billy, you sit next to Winkie. It should be boy-girl, boy-girl. That's it. [satisfied] Yes. Very good.

**PEGGY**
Now what do we do with our napkins? [waiting] ... [delighted] Yes. Exactly. We unfold them and tuck them under our chins. And then we put on our party hats.

**BREWSTER**
Can the boys wear their hats [genuinely curious, slightly smug] in the house?

**PEGGY**
Yes they can, Brewster, because this is a special occasion. And [warm] sometimes on special occasions, the rules can change.

**PEGGY**
I said [firm] sometimes. And I meant [pointed] some of the rules.

**BILLY**
There's my [excited, pointing] Daddy.

**PEGGY**
[quickly] Where, Billy?

**PEGGY**
[casual, slightly caught off guard] Oh. Hi.

**TED**
[casual, waving] Hi.

**PEGGY**
What brings you here?

**TED**
Have to pick up [matter-of-fact] Bill.

**PEGGY**
I thought [slightly confused] Judy was picking him up.

**TED**
She [neutral] asked me to.

**PEGGY**
You're a little early. [pointed] We haven't even had our cake.

**TED**
She told me [slightly defensive] to be early.

**SANDRA**
I can't get mine [whining, frustrated] to work.

**PEGGY**
Help her, Brewster. [instructing] Little boys are supposed to help little girls.

**TED**
Where's [probing] Frank?

**PEGGY**
Playing golf. [dry, bitter] Where else?

**TED**
On Winkie's [pointed] birthday?

**PEGGY**
Don't get me started. [exasperated] Please.

**WINKIE**
Can we have the ice cream [eager, impatient] now, please.

**PEGGY**
In a minute, dear. [distracted] Be patient. Then you'll have something to look forward to.

**TED**
Judy must have known [lowering voice] he'd be playing golf.

**PEGGY**
Judy knows [dry] everything.

**TED**
She knows about us, [careful, quiet] at least.

**PEGGY**
About us? [alarmed, hushed] How?

**TED**
She said she could tell [hushed] by the way we behaved.

**PEGGY**
Behaved? [confused] Where?

**TED**
At the [hushed] Bramwell's dinner party.

**PEGGY**
We [dismissive] hardly spoke to each other.

**TED**
That's [dry] how she could tell.

**PEGGY**
They want ice cream.

**TED**
She says she'll fight it, [holding her arm, urgent] tooth and nail.

**PEGGY**
Fight what? [sharp] We haven't done anything.

**TED**
She wants to [tense] nip it in the bud.

**PEGGY**
[loud, cheerful] All right, children. You win. Now Roberta is very busy in the kitchen because she also has a dinner party tonight. So who would like to help bring things out?

**PEGGY**
All right. Tell you what. Billy, you get the ice cream, and Sandra, [excited] you bring out the cake! [urgent] Careful, careful! Walk, don't run! And be polite to Roberta because she's working very hard. And Brewster and Winkie, [conspiratorial] you'll have other responsibilities! For instance, Brewster, when Billy and Sandra reappear through that door, [hushed] what will you do?

**BREWSTER**
[proud, slow] Sing the song.

**PEGGY**
[encouraging] Good, Brewster. Now be very quiet, and watch that door, and as soon as they come out, [quietly urgent] start singing.

**PEGGY**
So [urgent whisper] what do we do?

**TED**
She says she's thinking of telling [grave] her father about us.

**PEGGY**
Her [alarmed] father?

**TED**
He'd fire me. [grim] Immediately.

**PEGGY**
What if [quietly defiant] he did?

**TED**
I'd be out of a job, [bitter] Peggy.

**PEGGY**
You could [quietly defiant] get another.

**TED**
Where? [frustrated] Doing what?

**PEGGY**
No, no, sweetheart. [warm] Wait. Always wait. Before you blow out the candles, you have to make a wish. [quietly emotional] And Mummy has to make a wish. See? Mummy is putting her wedding ring around one of the candles. [soft] Now we both close our eyes and make a wish.

**WINKIE**
I wish I could have [excited, almost slipping]—

**PEGGY**
No, [gentle] no. Don't tell. Never tell a wish. [hushed] If you do, it won't come true. All right. [bright] Now blow.

**PEGGY**
Now Winkie, would you cut the cake and give everyone a piece, please. And Brewster, [cheerful] you pass the ice cream.

**TED**
What did you [quiet, careful] wish for?

**PEGGY**
[evasive] Won't tell.

**TED**
Do you think it will [hopeful, soft] come true?

**PEGGY**
[flat, sad] No.

**TED**
She'd make it [resigned] so messy. For everyone.

**PEGGY**
[quietly] Judy.

**TED**
She'd make it [insistent] impossible.

**PEGGY**
So would [resigned] Frank.

**TED**
I thought [surprised] he didn't care.

**PEGGY**
He'd care [quietly] if it were messy.

**TED**
We could [after a pause, lower] leave town.

**PEGGY**
And go [wistful] where?

**TED**
Wherever I find [practical] another job.

**PEGGY**
[quietly, weighing it] Yes...

**TED**
I've got an uncle [offering] in Syracuse.

**PEGGY**
[testing the word] Syracuse?

**TED**
We could [tentative] live there.

**PEGGY**
Is it [genuinely curious, grasping] nice? Syracuse?

**TED**
I think it's on [trying to sell it] some lake.

**PEGGY**
[quietly, distant] Syracuse...

**TED**
You'd have Winkie. [gently] I'd get Bill in the summer.

**PEGGY**
In [hollow] Syracuse.

**TED**
At least we'd [quietly] be free.

**PEGGY**
Winkie, [back to mother mode] wipe your mouth, please.

**TED**
[to Billy, firm but low] Billy.

**BILLY**
[confused] What?

**TED**
Would you come here [firm but low] a minute, please?

**TED**
Do you have to go [awkward, dad voice] the bathroom?

**BILLY**
[confused] No.

**TED**
Then [firm] don't do that, please.

**BILLY**
Don't do [genuinely baffled] what?

**TED**
You know what. Now go back and [slightly exasperated] enjoy the party.

**TED**
[quiet, wry] Sorry.

**PEGGY**
I [quietly pained] grew up here.

**TED**
Who [gently] didn't?

**PEGGY**
To just [voice breaking slightly] pick up stakes...

**TED**
[quietly] I know.

**PEGGY**
I mean, [soft, aching] this is where I live.

**TED**
Me, too.

**TED**
We'll just have to [touching her, soft] behave ourselves, then.

**PEGGY**
[overcome] Oh Ted...

**TED**
Be [ironic] good little children.

**PEGGY**
Oh [desperate] I can't stand it.

**TED**
And if we're seated next to each other, we'll have to make [careful] a conscious effort.

**PEGGY**
Oh we won't be seated next to each other. [dry, knowing] Judy will see to that.

**TED**
For a while, [resigned] anyway.

**PEGGY**
For [resigned] quite a while.

**WINKIE**
Everyone's finished, [proud] Mummy.

**PEGGY**
[tender] Thank you, sweetheart.

**WINKIE**
And here's your ring. [handing it over, sweet] From the cake.

**PEGGY**
Good for you, darling! [delighted, covering emotion] I forgot all about it!

**TED**
Time to go, [practical] then?

**PEGGY**
I've planned [brisk] some games.

**TED**
Want me [offering] to stay?

**PEGGY**
It would [simply] help.

**TED**
Then [easy] I'll stay.

**PEGGY**
Into the living room now, children. [cheerful] For some games.

**BREWSTER**
What [impatient] games?

**PEGGY**
Oh all kinds of games! [bright] Blind Man's Bluff. Pin the Tail on the Donkey...

**TED**
I'll get them [playful] started.

**PEGGY**
Would you? [laughing] While I propitiate Roberta.

**TED**
I'll be [smiling] the donkey.

**PEGGY**
[laughing] Oh stop.

**TED**
I'll be [leaning in] the ass.

**PEGGY**
Stop or [laughing, flustered] I'll scream.

**WINKIE**
Come on, [rushing in] Mummy! We're [urgent] waiting!

**PEGGY**
We're coming, [catching herself] dear.
